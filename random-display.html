May 12, 1959: C. William Edwards '36, Director of Admission, issues letters admitting us to Princeton.
Oct. 6, 1962: Bill Wright intercepts a pass from Columbia's quarterback, Archie Roberts, and returns it 72 yards for a touchdown - - still the longest scoring return by an interior lineman in the history of Princeton football. The play came as Columbia was driving for the tying touchdown just before the end of the first half. Coach Dick Colman later called it "the turning point" in Princeton's win.
Oct. 15, 1961: Dennis Keller and Peter Friend found the Student Pizza Agency, which quickly becomes a thriving enterprise. Sept. 15, 1959: 801 of us assemble in Alexander Hall to hear President Goheen proclaim us Princeton's finest class ever.
April 21, 1962: Teun Schoolwerth throws a no-hitter, one of only 13 in the history of Princeton baseball.
June 18, 1963: We assemble on the lawn in front of Nassau Hall to receive our degrees at Princeton's 216th Commencement. Walt Slocombe is our Valedictorian and Russell Carpenter is our Salutatorian.
June 30, 2020: Selden Edwards stands down as Class Secretary after 54 years of service with high distinction.
March 29, 1963: Gardiner Green wins NCAA championship in 100 yard breaststroke.
